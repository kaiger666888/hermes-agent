## Summary

The two foundational schools of film theory, systematized in Dudley Andrew's *Major Film Theories* (经典电影理论导论, Project -045):

- **Formalism tradition** (Part 1): Münsterberg → Arnheim → Eisenstein → Balázs
- **Realism tradition** (Part 2): Kracauer → Bazin

Hermes AI 短剧 production rarely needs to invoke these directly, but they underpin every visual / narrative choice. Use this ref when:
- Style genome needs theoretical anchors (e.g., is this 短剧 realist or formalist?)

## The Two-Tradition Matrix

### Formalism Tradition (Andrew Part 1)

**Core proposition:** Film is art because it *transforms* reality.

| Theorist | Core Concept | Key Work |
|---|---|---|
| **Hugo Münsterberg** (Ch.1) | Film follows mental processes; screen is extension of mind | *The Photoplay: A Psychological Study* (1916) |
| **Rudolf Arnheim** (Ch.2) | Film becomes art because of its *differences* from reality | *Film as Art* (1933) |
| **Sergei Eisenstein** (Ch.3) | Montage = conflict; rational cinema; A+B=C | *Film Form*, *Film Sense* |
| **Béla Balázs** (Ch.4) | Visual culture; the visible human; film as return to visual communication | *Der sichtbare Mensch* (1924) |

### Realism Tradition (Andrew Part 2)

**Core proposition:** Film's essence is *recording* reality.

| Theorist | Core Concept | Key Work |
|---|---|---|
| **Siegfried Kracauer** (Ch.5) | "Film is the redemption of physical reality"; film nature = photographic nature | *Theory of Film* (1960) |
| **André Bazin** (Ch.6) | Ontology of photographic image; myth of total cinema; deep focus; long take | *What Is Cinema?* |

## Binary Comparison Matrix

| Dimension | Formalism | Realism |
|---|---|---|
| **Medium view** | Medium limits are source of art | Medium's "recording" is essence |
| **Reality relation** | Transforms, abstracts, synthesizes | Reduces, records, reveals |
| **Rhythm** | Montage rhythm (Eisenstein) | In-frame rhythm (Bazin) |
| **Director** | Artist (strong authorship) | Revealer (humble author) |
| **Genre affinity** | Avant-garde, montage films, animation | Neo-realism, documentary, New Wave |
| **Representative directors** | Eisenstein, Hitchcock, Griffith | De Sica, Rossellini, Welles |
| **Signature techniques** | Close-up, fast cutting, low-angle, distortion | Deep focus, long take, natural light, location |

## French Avant-Garde Position (from 电影美学概述, Project -044)

**Photogénie** (Louis Delluc):
- The unique poetic quality of cinematic imagery
- "Lens grants objects a special quality, transcending the everyday"
- Photogénie exemplars: face close-ups, smoke, fog, light

**Rhythm School** (Germaine Dulac, Léon Moussinac):
- "Pure cinema"主张: film should be pure form, like music

**Myth of Total Cinema** (Bazin, traced in 电影理论史):
- "Humanity has long dreamed of an art that 'completely reproduces reality'; film is the realization of that dream"

## School Determination Method (5-Step)

For any given film / 短剧, determine its school:

1. **Cutting style:** Fast / conflict cutting → Formalism; Long take / deep focus → Realism
2. **Space handling:** Fragmented / collaged space → Formalism; Continuous space → Realism
3. **Acting:** Star system / performed → Formalism; Non-professional / natural → Realism
4. **Theme:** Idea expression → Formalism; Social observation → Realism
5. **Setting:** Studio / built sets → Formalism; Location / outdoor → Realism

### Boundary cases

- **Welles, *Citizen Kane*:** Deep focus (realism) + complex structure (formalism) — cross-school
- **Tarkovsky:** Long takes (realism) + highly poeticized (formalism) — cross-school
- **Hitchcock:** Realist technique (continuous space), formalist purpose (manipulating audience psychology)

## Application to AI 短剧

短剧 produced via Hermes pipeline tends toward **formalism** by default:
- Fast cutting (3-9s beat density)
- Built scenes (Blender previz)
- Performed (TTS voices, not natural)
- Idea-driven (爽点 / 钩子 / 卡点)

But successful 短剧 often *borrow realism signals*:
- Natural-light grading
- Handheld motion
- Genuine emotion anchors

Use this matrix to diagnose why a 短剧 "feels fake" — usually too much formalism, not enough realism anchor.
