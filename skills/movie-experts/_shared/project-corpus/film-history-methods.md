## 4 Historiographical Paths (from 电影史：理论与实践)

### Path 1: Aesthetic History
**Research question:** How did film evolve as art?
**Sources:** Films, criticism, director interviews
**Methods:** Work analysis, style evolution
**Representative:** Bazin *What Is Cinema?*

### Path 2: Economic History
**Research question:** How did film operate as an industry?
**Sources:** Studio archives, box office data, contracts
**Methods:** Statistical analysis, industry analysis
**Representative:** 好莱坞模式 (Project 057)

### Path 3: Technological History
**Research question:** How did film technology develop?
**Sources:** Patents, equipment, technical manuals
**Methods:** Technical analysis, impact assessment
**Representative:** 牛津世界电影史 technical chapters

### Path 4: Social History
**Research question:** How did film interact with society?
**Sources:** Audience research, media coverage, government documents
**Methods:** Audience analysis, cultural studies
**Representative:** Dai Hua film reviews

## Historiographical Methodology (from Ch.1 of 电影史：理论与实践)

### Empiricism
- History is "accumulation of facts"
- Sources come first
- Representative: early film history research

### Conventionalism
- History is "paradigm-driven"
- Question framework determines source selection
- Representative: Kuhn's *Structure of Scientific Revolutions* influence

### Realism
- History is "mechanism revelation"
- Neither pure fact nor pure paradigm
- Seeks "mechanisms that produce phenomena"
- **Book authors' recommended path**

## 5-Step Film History Research Method

### Step 1: Define the question
- NOT "study 1950s cinema"
- BUT "why did 1950s Hollywood adopt wide-screen?"

### Step 2: Choose the path
- Aesthetic / Economic / Technological / Social
- Different paths determine sources and methods

### Step 3: Collect sources
- Primary: films, archives, interviews
- Secondary: research works, papers

### Step 4: Analyze and explain
- Propose causal mechanisms
- Distinguish correlation from causation

### Step 5: Write narrative
- Choose narrative mode (chronological / question-driven / comparative)
- Mind historiographical ethics

---

## Primary Source Types (from Ch.3-5)

### Film evidence
- Films themselves
- Scripts, storyboards
- Production logs
- Posters, stills

### Non-film evidence
- Studio archives
- Government documents
- Press / media
- Personal diaries, letters
- Oral history

## Application to AI 短剧

短剧 itself is becoming a film-historical research subject. For academic / compliance research:

### 4 paths for studying 短剧
1. **Aesthetic history** — How did 短剧 visual style emerge?
2. **Economic history** — Platform economics, paid conversion
3. **Technological history** — From live-action to AI-generated
4. **Social history** — 抖音 / 快手 algorithmic culture

### Each path's source materials
- **Aesthetic**: 短剧 themselves, beat sheets, director interviews
- **Economic**: Platform revenue data, 付费卡点 placement
- **Technological**: AI model release timeline, AIGC tools
- **Social**: User research, social media discourse, government regulation

### Realism-path research questions
- "What mechanisms produce 爆款 短剧?"
- "What causal factors distinguish 小程序剧 from 抖音 短剧?"
- "Why did 男频 decline relative to 女频 in 2024-2026?"
