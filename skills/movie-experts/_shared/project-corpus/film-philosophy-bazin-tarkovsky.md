## André Bazin — Realist Ontology

### Core Concepts (from 电影是什么)

#### 1. Ontology of the Photographic Image (摄影影像本体论)
- The photographic image shares an *ontological* bond with its subject
- Unlike painting, photography is "automatically" produced — the photographer does not "create" the image, the lens does
- This automaticity gives photography its special epistemic status

#### 2. Myth of Total Cinema (完整电影神话)
- "Humanity has dreamt since antiquity of an art that completely reproduces reality"
- Cinema is the *fulfillment* of this dream, not its invention
- Sound, color, 3D, wide-screen — all are steps toward "total cinema"

#### 3. Mummy Complex (木乃伊情意综)
- From Bazin's *电影是什么* Vol. 1
- Humanity's deepest desire: to *preserve* life against time
- Egyptian mummification → portraiture → photography → cinema
- Each medium is a new form of "mummification"

#### 4. Long Take & Deep Focus (长镜头与景深)
- Bazin's *prescriptive* conclusion from his ontology
- Montage distorts reality by forcing relationships
- Deep focus + long take *respect* spatial-temporal continuity
- Director chooses *what* to show, not *how to construct* it

#### 5. Evolution of Film Language
- Bazin argued film "evolved" toward realism
- Welles, Renoir, De Sica = "advanced" stage
- Montage cinema (Eisenstein) = "primitive" stage

### Bazin's Canonical Films
- **Welles** *Citizen Kane* (1941) — deep focus
- **Renoir** *Rules of the Game* (1939) — deep space
- **De Sica** *Bicycle Thieves* (1948) — neo-realism
- **Rossellini** *Rome, Open City* (1945) — neo-realism

### Bazin's Limits (Modern Critique)
- Over-values realism as "evolution"
- Under-values montage's expressive power
- Neglects avant-garde / formalist traditions
- Yet remains the *strongest* theoretical foundation for documentary / realist filmmaking

## Andrei Tarkovsky — Sculpting Time

### Core Concepts (from 雕刻时光, Project -022)

#### 1. Sculpting in Time (雕刻时光)
- "Cinema is the art of sculpting in time"
- Director does not "tell a story" — they *shape time itself*
- Time passes differently in cinema than in reality
- Long takes are not "Bazin's respect for reality" — they are Tarkovsky's *pressure* on time

#### 2. Film Image (电影影像)
- Not the same as "photographic image"
- Film image is *observational* — the camera watches, time accumulates
- Image must have *inner time*, not just external representation

#### 3. Cinema's Fated Role (电影的宿命角色)
- Tarkovsky: cinema has a spiritual mission
- Art is a "rite" — connects human to transcendent
- Anti-formalist: structure should emerge from spiritual necessity, not formal calculation

#### 4. Poetry in Cinema
- Cinema should be *poetic*, not *prosaic*
- "Prosaic" = Hollywood narrative logic
- "Poetic" = associative, image-driven, dream-like

### Tarkovsky's Films (七部半 = 7.5 films)

| Year | Film | Duration | Key |

| 1961 | *The Steamroller and the Violin* (short = 0.5) | 45 min | Student film |

### Tarkovsky's Style Fingerprint
- Extremely long takes (often 5-10 minutes)
- Slow camera movement (almost imperceptible)
- Water imagery (rain, streams, pools)
- Animals (dogs, horses)
- Decay & ruin
- Religious iconography

## Application to AI 短剧

短剧 pipeline rarely produces Tarkovsky-style work — the runtime constraint (60-180s per episode) is antithetical to sculpting-time. But these concepts help:

### Bazin in 短剧
- When a 短剧 *feels* fake, check if it breaks Bazin's ontology
- Deep focus in a key shot → anchors viewer in space
- Long take in the climax beat → "lets the moment breathe"

### Tarkovsky in 短剧
- The *anchor shot* technique: a 3-5s static observation within an otherwise fast-cut 短剧
- Water / nature imagery as "spiritual punctuation"
- Slow motion in climax (cf. Tarkovsky's *Stalker*)

### Diagnostic questions for `editor` / `cinematographer`
- Is this scene *montage* (Eisenstein) or *mise-en-scène* (Bazin)?
- Are cuts *conflict-driven* or *time-pressure-driven*?
- Does the long take serve *observation* (Tarkovsky) or *delay* (manipulation)?
