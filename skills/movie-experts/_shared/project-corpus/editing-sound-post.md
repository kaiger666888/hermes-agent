## Part 1: 剪辑之道 — Murch in Conversation (Project 071)

### Book structure (verified)
**5 cities, 5 conversations:**
- 对话｜壹 旧金山 (San Francisco)
- 对话｜贰 洛杉矶 (Los Angeles)
- 对话｜叁 纽约城 (New York City)
- 对话｜肆 旧金山 (San Francisco)
- 对话｜终结篇 多伦多 (Toronto)

### Core proposition: "Humble Voice" (谦卑的声音)
- Editor is not "creator"
- Editor is "revealer"
- Film "already exists in the material"
- Editor just "makes it appear"

**Contrast with "father of editing":**
- Kuleshov / Eisenstein: editor as "creator"
- Murch: editor as "listener to material"

### Murch's 6 Editing Principles (with weights)

| Principle | Weight |
|---|---|
| **Emotion (51%)** | Most important |
| **Story (23%)** | Secondary |
| **Rhythm (10%)** | Third |
| **Eye-trace (7%)** | Fourth |
| **2D plane (5%)** | Fifth |
| **3D space (4%)** | Sixth |

**Meaning:**
- Cut point choice is **51% decided by emotion**
- Story, rhythm next
- Technical (plane/space) least

### Murch's 5 Editing Decision Points

#### 1. The Blink (眨眼)
- Actor's blink often natural cut point
- Audience blink also a rhythm signal
- See also *In the Blink of an Eye* (not in project)

#### 2. Dream & Cinema
- Cinema like dream (dark room, projection)
- Cutting rhythm like dream rhythm

#### 3. Time & Material
- Editor is "sculptor of time"
- Delete, compress, reorganize

#### 4. Medium Archaeology
- Murch studies medium history
- Borrows from silent film

#### 5. Digital vs Film Editing
- Digital advantage: low attempt cost
- Digital disadvantage: too many choices, hard to decide

### Murch workflow

#### Stage 1: Assembly (粗剪)
- Don't pursue perfection
- Let material "speak for itself"
- No preset structure

#### Stage 2: Rough → Fine Cut (精剪)
- Find rhythm
- Delete redundancy
- Strengthen emotion

#### Stage 3: Picture Lock (定剪)
- No more picture changes
- Move to sound mixing

#### Stage 4: Mixing (混录)
- Murch is also sound designer
- *Apocalypse Now*, *The English Patient*

### Murch's 2.5/14/30 Rule
- **2.5:** Ideal cut point distance is 2.5 frames
- **14:** Audience doesn't "notice" cuts within 14 frames
- **30:** Audience starts "noticing" after 30 frames

**Meaning:**
- Good cut is "invisible"
- Missed cut makes audience "exit the film"

### Editor's role definition
**Book core proposition (verified):**
> "Editor is the director's 'other self'"

**Meaning:**
- Director too close to material, needs editor's "distance"
- Editor is "objective eye"
- While understanding director's intent

### Murch's "Pao Ding Jie Niu" (庖丁解牛) metaphor
- Editor should be like Pao Ding (master butcher)
- Use "spiritual encounter" not "visual observation"
- Follow material's grain
- Knife (editing tool) doesn't dull

### Classic Murch Works

| Film | Murch Contribution |
|---|---|
| *Apocalypse Now* | Editing + sound design |
| *The Conversation* | Editing + sound design |
| *The English Patient* | Editing (Oscar) |
| *Cold Blood* | Editing |
| *Ghost* | Editing |
| *The Talented Mr. Ripley* | Editing |

## Part 3: 音效圣经 — Sound Effects Bible (Project 032)

### Book positioning
"Vazquez's sound bible" — Detroit Rib House & Blastwave FX

### Verified structure
- 推荐序一 (Charles Maynes)
- 推荐序二 (Yao Guoqiang)
- About Detroit Rib House & Blastwave FX
- 1.1 Hard effects
- 1.2 Foley effects
- 1.3 Background effects
- 1.4 Electronic effects / elements
- 1.5 Sound design effects
- 录音十诫 (Ten Commandments of Recording)

### 5 Sound Effect Categories

#### 1. Hard Effects (硬效果)
- Sync with picture, specific sound
- From sound library typically
- Examples: gun, door slam, car engine

**Production:**
- Time-precise sync
- Choose realistic sound
- Multi-layer for enhancement

#### 2. Foley Effects (拟音)
- Performed in studio in real-time
- Mainly footsteps, clothing, props
- Named after Jack Foley

**Production:**
- Different floor materials (wood / concrete / grass)
- Different shoe types
- Clothing materials (silk / cotton / leather)

#### 3. Background Effects (背景效果 / Ambience)
- Invisible environmental atmosphere
- Multi-layer (far / mid / near)
- Examples: city traffic, restaurant chatter, forest

**Production:**
- At least 3 layers (far / mid / near)
- Stereo or surround
- Match scene mood

#### 4. Electronic Effects (电子音效)
- Synthesized / sampled
- Non-realistic
- Suitable for sci-fi, horror
- Examples: alien voice, machine sound

**Production:**
- Modular synth
- Sample pitch-shift
- FM synthesis

#### 5. Sound Design Effects (声音设计)
- Comprehensive designed special sound
- Unidentifiable source
- Narrative / emotional service
- Examples: *Star Wars* lightsaber, *Inception* BWONG

**Production:**
- Multi-layer mix
- Frequency design
- Music integration

### Recording Ten Commandments (录音十诫)

1. Never touch microphone with bare hands
2. Never ignore monitoring (must wear headphones)
3. Never ignore levels (avoid clipping)
4. Never single-take recording (must sample multiple)
5. Never ignore environment (avoid unwanted noise)
6. Never lack wind protection (outdoor must use windscreen)
7. Never ignore phase (multi-mic attention)
8. Never lack backup (dual-track recording)
9. Never ignore file management (precise naming)
10. Never forget log (record each take's info)

### Sound library management

#### File naming convention
```
[Category]_[Subcategory]_[Description]_[Version]_[Format].wav
Example: FOOTSTEPS_WOOD_MALE_WALK_01_48k24b.wav
```

#### Classification
- By category (5 main)
- By scene (home / office / outdoor)
- By emotion (tense / relaxed / horror)

### 5 Sound Design Layers

#### Layer 1: Real
- Audible specific sounds
- Sync hard effects + foley

#### Layer 2: Environmental
- Invisible atmosphere
- Background effects

#### Layer 3: Emotional
- Express character inner
- Sound design effects

#### Layer 4: Metaphorical
- Hint at theme
- Recurring sound motif

#### Layer 5: Subconscious
- Audience can't hear but feel
- Subsonic, low frequency

### Classic sound design cases

| Film | Innovation |
|---|---|
| *Star Wars* lightsaber | TV noise + film projector |
| *Jurassic Park* T-Rex | Whale + tiger + elephant mix |
| *Inception* BWONG | Slowed Édith Piaf song |
| *Apocalypse Now* helicopter | Helicopter + fan rhythm |
| *LOTR* Nazgûl scream | Epoxy-coated teacup friction |

## Part 4: 视听：幻觉的构建 — Audio-Vision (Project 060)

### Book positioning
Michel Chion's *Audio-Vision: Sound on Screen* — "Most in-depth comprehensive study of film sound"

### Chion's key concepts (synthesized)

#### 1. Sound flow logic
- Internal logic: sound's own consistency
- External logic: sound's relationship to image

#### 2. Three sound types
- **Diegetic (画内):** in story world, characters hear
- **Non-diegetic (画外):** outside story, only audience hears (e.g., background music)
- **Non-narrative (非叙境):** pure form (e.g., abstract electronic)

#### 3. Sound as multi-stage rocket
- Primary sound: dominant layer
- Secondary: supporting layer
- Tertiary: atmospheric layer

#### 4. Relative vs absolute off-screen space
- **Relative:** defined by what's on-screen
- **Absolute:** beyond any frame

### Chion's audio-vision 5 modes
1. **Added value** — sound adds to image
2. **Synchresis** — sound-image sync perception
3. **Punctualization** — sound anchors image
4. **Counterpoint** — sound-image contrast
5. **Substitution** — sound replaces image

## Part 5: 纪录片创作六讲 — Documentary Methods (Project 049)

### Book positioning
Wang Jing (王竞), Beijing Film Academy professor

### Verified structure (6 lectures)

#### Lecture 1: Why make documentaries?
- Documentary types
- From material to film
- Network era video
- Conditions for public video
- Subject selection

#### Lecture 2: Film-Eye (电影—眼睛)
- Dziga Vertov's "Kino-Eye" theory
- Camera as "mechanical eye"
- "Sees" what ordinary people cannot

#### Lecture 3: Hero Nanook (主人公纳努克)
- Flaherty's *Nanook of the North*
- Documentary ethics
- Boundary between staging and reality

#### Lecture 4: Point of View (观点)
- Documentary must have a point of view
- "Objectivity" is a myth
- Grierson model

#### Lecture 5: Subject (题材)
- Subject selection determines everything
- Subject's "weight"

#### Lecture 6: Method (方法)
- Shooting method
- Cutting method
- Completion method

### 6 Documentary Types

| Type | Feature | Representative |
|---|---|---|
| **Poetic** | Rhythm, form | Ivens *Rain* |
| **Expository** | Omniscient narration | Grierson model |
| **Observational** | "Fly on the wall" | *Primary* |
| **Participatory** | Filmmaker in frame | Rouch *Chronicle of a Summer* |
| **Reflexive** | Filmmaking process in frame | *Man with a Movie Camera* |
| **Performative** | Personal memory | *The Gleaners and I* |

### 4 Documentary Schools

#### 1. Flaherty model (*Nanook*)
- Long-term co-presence + staging
- Subjects "cooperate" to perform daily life
- Staging vs reality ethics

#### 2. Vertov "Kino-Eye" (Lecture 2)
- Camera keener than human eye
- Capture "invisible truth"
- Anti-staging, anti-narrative

#### 3. Grierson model (Lecture 4)
- "Documentary is the creative treatment of reality"
- Omniscient narration
- Educational, social-issue driven

#### 4. Direct Cinema / Cinéma Vérité
**Direct Cinema (US):**
- "Fly on the wall"
- No intervention, no narration
- Robert Drew *Primary*

**Cinéma Vérité (France):**
- Filmmaker enters frame
- Triggers conversation
- Rouch *Chronicle of a Summer*

### Subject selection "5 Cs"

1. **Consequence:** Is this important?
2. **Conflict:** Is there conflict?
3. **Character:** Are characters vivid?
4. **Context:** Is there social context?
5. **Change:** Will filming produce change?

### Documentary ethics

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| Informed consent | Subjects understand purpose |
| Authenticity | No fabrication for drama |
| Power dynamics | Beware filmmaker's power |
| Privacy boundary | Don't film what subject doesn't want |
| Post-production feedback | Show subjects the finished film |

## Application to AI 短剧

### Murch for `editor`
- Apply 6-rule weights in `editor`'s LLM prompt
- "Humble voice" = AI should respect source material, not over-impose
- 2.5/14/30 rule for cut-point distance

### Sound Bible for `audio_pipeline` (foley / mixer sub-steps)
- 5-category classification for sound asset taxonomy
- 10 commandments as recording quality gate
- 5 layers for emotional sound design

### Chion for `audio_pipeline` (composer / spatial_audio sub-steps)
- 5 audio-vision modes for music-image relationships
- Synchresis = AI music must sync visual accents
- Added value = music should add meaning, not double image

### Documentary methods for `screenplay` (docu-style 短剧)
- 短剧 increasingly borrows documentary aesthetics (POV, real-feel)
- 5Cs subject selection for 短剧 IP choice
- Direct cinema style = trending "真实感" 短剧 format
