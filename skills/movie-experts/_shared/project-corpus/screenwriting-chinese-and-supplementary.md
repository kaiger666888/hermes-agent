## 1. Lu Wei (芦苇) — Chinese Epic Adaptation Method

### From 《白鹿原》芦苇电影剧本+剧本创作笔记

**Lu Wei verified identity:** screenwriter of *Farewell My Concubine* (霸王别姬), *To Live* (活着), *Tuya's Marriage* (图雅的婚事)

### Lu Wei's core principles

#### Seven drafts over five years
**Book quote (verified):**
> "七易其稿，历时五载，独家访谈揭秘创作始末"

**Implication:**
- Epic scripts are *revised* into existence, not written
- Each revision is "re-understanding"
- Don't fear rewrites — good scripts need 5-10 drafts

#### Highly cinematic adaptation
**Lu Wei's adaptation principles:**
- Long novels cannot be "transplanted whole"; must *select*
- Selection criterion: **dramatic function vs literary value**
- Cut novel's poetic descriptions, preserve dramatic action
- Convert "inner monologue" to "visible action"

#### Genre conventions
**Lu Wei quote (verified):**
> "《白鹿原》是一部扎实的史诗片剧作范本，无论是剧情还是人物都符合类型片创作规范"

**Implication:**
- Even art films should follow genre conventions
- Genre = contract between audience and creator
- Epic genre conventions: ensemble, era-spanning, family, historical pivot

#### Dialect polishing
**Lu Wei detail (verified):**
> "在第七稿定稿上又多次校订细节，尤其是雕琢人物台词的方言与措辞"

**Implication:**
- Dialect is not decoration, it's character identity
- Phrasing needs "era + region + class" feel
- Dialect dialogue is the soul of epic film

### Lu Wei's epic adaptation workflow
```
1. Read source novel (multiple times)
2. Theme refinement (core of the film)
3. Selection list (keep / cut / merge)
4. Protagonist decision (epic POV problem)
5. Structure building (era span + three acts)
6. Scene writing (dramatic function of each scene)
7. Dialect dialogue (regionalization)
8. Multi-draft revision (≥5 drafts)
```

### Application to 短剧 IP adaptation
- 短剧 often adapts web novels — Lu Wei's method applies
- Selection > transcription
- Dialect (or 平台方言) = 短剧 differentiation

## 2. Viki King — 21-Day Screenplay Method

### From 《21天搞定电影剧本》(Project 009)

**Book positioning:** "The most concise and practical screenwriting handbook / action guide from prep to finished script"

### Core method: Heart vs Brain

#### Viki King's central proposition (verified from Ch.2)
> "2.1 怎么让你的脑听从于心" (How to make your brain listen to your heart)

**Meaning:**
- **Brain**: logic, planning, criticism, obstacle
- **Heart**: intuition, emotion, creation, flow
- Good scripts come from "heart", "brain" for revision

### Heart vs Brain task division

| Task | Heart or Brain? |
|---|---|
| Find story | Heart |
| Write first draft | Heart |
| Revise | Brain |
| Check structure | Brain |
| Write dialogue | Heart |
| Check dialogue | Brain |

### 3 Unique Tools

#### Tool 1: 9-Minute Movie (9 分钟电影路标)
- 9-minute eyes-closed meditation
- Whole film plays in "fast forward" in mind
- Record key images / moments
- Purpose: capture film's "skeleton"

#### Tool 2: 2-Page Picture Show (2 页图示)
- 2 pages drawing the whole film
- Page 1: story structure (beginning/middle/end)
- Page 2: character relationships
- Purpose: visualize whole film

#### Tool 3: 8-Minute Author (8 分钟作者)
- 8 minutes writing "who you are, why you write this story"
- Purpose: dialogue with self, find "must write" reason
- Age will leak your thinking

### 21-Day Schedule

**Week 1: Preparation (Day 1-7)**
- Day 1: Write "why write this story"
- Day 2: 9-Minute Movie
- Day 3: 2-Page Picture Show
- Day 4: 8-Minute Author
- Day 5-7: Character bio + outline

**Week 2: First draft (Day 8-14)**
- Day 8: Write Scene 1
- Day 9: Write Act 1 (30 pages)
- Day 10: Write Act 1-2 transition
- Day 11: Write Act 2A (30-60 pages)
- Day 12: Write Act 2B (60-90 pages)
- Day 13: Write Act 3 (90-120 pages)
- Day 14: Write ending

**Week 3: Revision (Day 15-21)**
- Day 15: Read whole draft
- Day 16: Revise Act 1
- Day 17: Revise Act 2
- Day 18: Revise Act 3
- Day 19: Revise dialogue
- Day 20: Format / spelling
- Day 21: Final draft

### Psychological counsel (from Ch.2.3)
**Viki King's diagnoses:**
- "Can't write" = brain too dominant
- "Writing too slow" = perfectionism
- "Stuck in middle" = haven't found "heart"

**Countermeasures:**
- Write first, revise later — first draft allowed to be bad
- Timed writing — 30 min per scene
- Don't re-read while writing — turn page immediately
- Don't look back

### Application to AI 短剧
- AI can adopt "heart first" mode — generate drafts without criticism
- "Brain" pass = editor / auditor refinement
- 21-day schedule maps to AI: draft (1 day) → polish (1 day) → audit (1 day)

## 3. Liu Tianci (刘天赐) — Theme "Cleverness"

### From 《编剧秘笈》(Project -006)

**Book structure (verified):**
- 前言
- 第一章 主题
  - (一) 主题是什么？
  - (二) 四种情况
  - (三) 主题是怎样决定的呢？
  - (四) 正确的主题思想
  - (五) 如何表现主题思想呢？
  - (六) 主题的"机灵性"

### Theme's "Cleverness" (机灵性)
**Core concept:**
- Theme should not be preachy
- Theme should "cleverly" embed in action
- Audience absorbs theme through plot, not dialogue

### Four situations for theme
1. For self (为自己)
2. For director (为导演)
3. For actor (为演员)
4. For audience (为观众)

### How to express theme
- Through action, not dialogue
- Through conflict, not exposition
- Through consequences, not statements
- Through character change, not narrator

## 4. Hook Design (钩子) — from 编剧的策略

### From 《编剧的策略》(Project 065)

**Book structure (verified):**
- 1.1 剧本是电影项目包的一部分
- **1.2 钩子** (Hook) — core concept
- **1.3 没钩子的片子是怎样拍成的** (How films without hooks get made)
- **1.4 钩子是怎么来的** (Where hooks come from)

### Hook's 4 sources
1. **Concept hook (high concept)** — one-line novel idea
   - *Groundhog Day* = reliving same day
   - *Inception* = entering others' dreams
2. **Character hook** — extreme/special character
   - *Rain Man* = autistic savant
   - *Schindler's List* = Nazi saving Jews
3. **Situation hook** — unusual circumstance
   - *Shawshank Redemption* = prison break
   - *2012* = end of the world
4. **Conflict hook** — built-in contradiction
   - *Godfather* = son who hates family business becomes godfather
   - *Avatar* = spy falls for natives

### 3-stage hook structure
1. **Hook introduction** (first 10 min) — one-sentence tells audience what story this is
2. **Hook development** (10-90 min) — unfold around hook
3. **Hook payoff** (last 30 min) — let hook "bloom"

### "Elevator pitch" test
- Tell hook in 30 seconds
- If listener says "oh" → hook failed
- If listener says "wow, then what?" → hook succeeded

### "Poster test"
- Can hook make a poster?
- Does poster make you want to see?

### Application to 短剧
短剧 hook = opening 3 seconds. Per [`hook_retention`](../../hook_retention/SKILL.md) expert:
- 钩子 must be in first 0-3s
- 钩子 must connect to 爽点 / 卡点 chain
- 钩子 must be platform-specific (抖音 vs 快手 vs 小程序剧)

## 5. Dan O'Bannon — Dynamic Structure

### From 《剧本结构设计》(Project 069)

**Book author verified:** Screenwriter of *Alien* (异形), 35-year career

### Core concept: Dynamic Structure
**O'Bannon's quote (verified):**
> "Dynamic structure" — script structure is not static "template", but "dynamic tension system"

**Meaning:**
- Three-act is skeleton
- Real vitality comes from "non-return point" tension
- Different genres need different structures

### Three "Non-Return Points"

#### Non-Return Point 1 (end of Act 1)
**Position:** End of Act 1
**Function:**
- Protagonist enters "no turning back" situation
- Accepts mission / makes decision
- Example: *Alien* crew deciding to open airlock

#### Non-Return Point 2: Midpoint (middle of Act 2)
**Position:** Middle of Act 2
**Function:**
- Theme reversal / information reveal
- Protagonist: passive → active
- Example: *Alien* — alien first appears

#### Non-Return Point 3 (end of Act 2)
**Position:** End of Act 2
**Function:**
- Maximum crisis
- Ready for final showdown
- Example: *Alien* — protagonist decides to destroy ship

### Multi-act structure experiments (book chapter "独幕，两幕，五幕")
- **One-act** — short films, single scene
- **Two-act** — minimal story, strong contrast
- **Three-act** — standard 90% of feature films
- **Five-act** — epic, Shakespeare

### O'Bannon vs Syd Field

| Dimension | Syd Field | O'Bannon |
|---|---|---|
| **Core** | Three-act fixed | Dynamic multi-act |
| **Method** | Plot points | Non-return points |
| **Flexibility** | Low | High |
| **Genre** | Universal | Genre films (horror, sci-fi) |

### Application to 短剧
短剧 runtime (60-180s) forces **two-act or one-act** structure:
- One-act: 60s 单集
- Two-act: 180s 单集 with midpoint reversal
- "Non-return point" in 短剧 = 卡点 (paywall cliffhanger)

## 6. Winston — Script as Literature

### From 《作为文学的电影剧本》(Project -002)

**Book structure (verified):**
- 第一章 电影是什么？
- 第二章 什么是文学？
- 第三章 本书的研究方法
- **第四章 电影文法**
- **第五章 镜头的调子：情绪上的和心理上的**
- **第六章 结构的各种形式**
- 第七章 电影的结构
- 第八章 电影的总体
- 第九章 剧作
- 第十章 小说对电影的影响

### Three Core Concepts

#### Film Grammar (Ch.4)
- Film has "grammar" like language
- Single shot = word
- Shot sequence = sentence
- Scene = paragraph
- Sequence = chapter

#### Shot Tone (Ch.5)
- Every shot has a "tone"
- Tone comes from: light, color, motion, sound
- Tone determines audience's emotional response

#### Structure Forms (Ch.6-7)
- Linear
- Circular
- Polyphonic
- Framed (story within story)
- Fragmented

### Application to 短剧
- 短剧 shot sequence ≈ "sentence"
- 短剧 scene ≈ "paragraph"
- "Shot tone" diagnosis: does each shot's tone match intended emotion?
