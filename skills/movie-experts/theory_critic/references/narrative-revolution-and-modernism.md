### Narrative Revolution Basic Vocabulary (Ch.2)

#### Core concepts
- **Non-linear (非线性):** break temporal order
- **Multi-perspective (多视角):** no single narrator
- **Open ending (开放结尾):** no closure
- **Fragmentation (碎片化):** parts > whole
- **Meta-narrative (元叙事):** expose narrative itself
- **Intertextuality (互文性):** dialogue with other texts

#### Narrative revolution sources
- French New Wave (Godard, Truffaut)
- Italian Neo-realism (partial)
- Modernist cinema (Antonioni, Fellini)
- Post-modern cinema (Tarantino, Lynch)

### What Narrative Revolution Opposes (Ch.3)

#### Opposed "classical narrative" concepts
- **Three-act structure:** fixed beginning-development-ending
- **Single protagonist:** one hero
- **Causal logic:** A causes B
- **Linear time:** time front-to-back
- **Omniscient POV:** god's eye view
- **Dramatic arc:** setup-conflict-resolution
- **Genre expectation:** satisfy audience expectation

#### Reasons for opposition
- Reality is not linear
- Real characters don't have "goals"
- Causality is constructed
- Audiences deserve challenge

### Narrative Revolution Related Vocabulary (Ch.4)

#### 1. Literary theory
- Barthes "death of author"
- Foucault "discourse theory"
- Derrida "différance"
- Deleuze "rhizome"

#### 2. Philosophy
- Phenomenology (Merleau-Ponty)
- Existentialism (Sartre)
- Post-structuralism

#### 3. Psychoanalysis
- Lacan "mirror stage"
- Freud "unconscious"

### Classic Narrative Revolution Films

| Film | Revolution Method |
|---|---|
| *Citizen Kane* | Multi-POV, flashback |
| *8½* | Meta-narrative |
| *Blow-Up* | Open ending |
| *Pulp Fiction* | Time disrupted |
| *Memento* | Reverse chronology |
| *Mulholland Drive* | Dream/reality mix |
| *Tropical Malady* | Two-part structure |

### 5 Methods for Narrative Revolution

#### Method 1: Break time
- Flashback, flashforward, insertion
- Multiple timelines
- Time loop

#### Method 2: Break perspective
- Multiple narrators
- Unreliable narrator
- Camera as narrator

#### Method 3: Break causality
- No clear causality
- Contingency dominates
- Multiple possibilities

#### Method 4: Break identity
- Character identity blurred
- Double identity
- Character swap

#### Method 5: Break meta-level
- Meta-cinema (expose filmmaking)
- Audience enters story
- Interactive narrative

## Part 2: Benjamin — Work of Art in Age of Mechanical Reproduction

### From 现代性的美学经验 (Project -072)

### Core propositions

#### 1. Aura (灵光)
- Artwork's aura = distance, uniqueness, here-and-now
- Mechanical reproduction (photography, cinema) destroys aura
- Yet this **liberates** art for the masses

#### 2. Distraction (心不在焉 / Zerstreuung)
- Cinema audience perception is "distracted"
- Unlike painting's concentrated contemplation
- Distracted perception suits modern masses

#### 3. Shock (震惊 / Schock)
- Rapid cutting creates shock
- Like urban life's shocks
- Cinema trains masses for modernity

### Benjamin vs Adorno

**Debate:** Is mass culture emancipatory or controlling?

**Benjamin's position:**
- Mass culture has emancipatory potential
- Mechanical reproduction breaks authority
- Cinema can educate masses

**Adorno's position:**
- Mass culture is "culture industry"
- Surface emancipation, actual control
- Hollywood is homogenizing machine

## Part 3: Adorno — Culture Industry

### Core propositions

#### 1. Culture industry (文化工业)
- Standardized, pseudo-individualized
- Surface variety, actual sameness
- Hollywood = culture industry paradigm

#### 2. Half-education (半教育 / Halbbildung)
- Surface education, actual dumbing-down
- Mass culture produces "informed but uncritical" audiences

#### 3. Negative dialectics
- Art should negate reality
- Anti "art for art's sake"
- Art's role: critique the status quo

### Adorno on cinema
- Pessimistic about Hollywood
- Optimistic about modernist film (Beckett)
- Against pure entertainment
- For art's critical function

## Application to AI 短剧

### Narrative revolution in 短剧
- 短剧 is mostly classical (linear, causal, single-POV)
- But *successful* 短剧 often borrow revolution techniques:
  - **Time disruption:** non-linear 爆款 formula
  - **Multiple POV:** ensemble 短剧
  - **Open ending:** 小程序剧 卡点 structure
- Use this ref to **diagnose** why a 短剧 IP feels fresh vs clichéd

### Critical theory for platform critique
- Apply Adorno's "culture industry" to 抖音 / 快手 algorithmic feeds
- Algorithmic feeds = standardized, pseudo-individualized
- 爆款 formula = Adorno's standardization critique
- Yet: 短剧 also has emancipatory moments (independent creators, niche sub-genres)

### Benjamin's aura in AI 短剧
- AI-generated 短剧 by definition lacks aura (mechanical reproduction)
- Yet some 短剧 IP cultivate aura through:
  - Recurring "sinthome" elements
  - Auteur-style visual signatures
  - Loyal audience relationships

### Mulvey test (from `psychoanalytic-film-theory.md`)
- Apply to 短剧 to identify gaze structure
- 男频 短剧 = explicit Mulvey-style
- 女频 短剧 invert gaze
- 现代短剧 increasingly use shared gaze
