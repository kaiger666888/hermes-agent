- McKee's scene-value-shift model: every scene MUST shift at least one value (life/death, love/hate, freedom/captivity, hope/despair, truth/lie) from positive to negative or vice versa. Value-shift rate: ≥ 1 value shift per scene. Give 5 canonical value-pair examples.
- McKee's BEAT definition: smallest unit of action-reaction. A 90s 短剧 scene typically has 3-5 beats. Provide concrete beat-decomposition for one full 90s scene.
- Turning Point vs Plot Point distinction: Turning Point = single beat that opens a gap; Plot Point = scene-level event that spins the story into a new act. 短剧 uses Plot Points at ~25% and ~75% runtime.

- Tan's "interest" structure: viewer interest = (concern for protagonist) × (uncertainty about outcome) × (anticipation of appraisal). Quantitative proxy: each scene should sustain concern-uncertainty-anticipation score ≥ 0.6 on a normalized scale.

- Empirical viewer-attention decay curve: attention drops ≥ 15% at 8-12s without a 击中点; drops ≥ 30% at 30-40s without a value-shift. Cite as base for 击中点 density rule (cross-link conflict-escalation.md).
