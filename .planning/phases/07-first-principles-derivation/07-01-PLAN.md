**§2.1 — Q1: What does the audience ultimately consume?**

- **Question framing** (1 paragraph CN prose): why this question is irreducible — it sets the teleological anchor. Everything downstream (which nodes? which AIGC transformations? which critics?) is justified by what the audience actually receives.

- **Sub-questions** (3-5 bullet points): decompose Q1 into smaller investigable angles. Examples: "Is the consumed artifact a story, an image sequence, an emotional arc, or all three?" "At what layer does the audience's experience occur — narrative, perceptual, emotional?" "Does the answer differ between 短剧 and 微电影?"

- **Corpus subset citation** (per DERIV-07 — RESEARCH §6 + STACK §1.4):
  - Primary: `01-剧本/` (17 narrative-intent files in skills-影视创作) + `06-理论批评/{cinema-fundamentals, film-philosophy-bazin, film-philosophy-tarkovsky}` (what cinema IS) + book 劳逊《戏剧与电影的剧作理论与技巧》
  - Secondary: `case-studies/case-01-短片创作全流程.md`
  - Hermes-integrated ready-to-cite: `theory-formalism-vs-realism.md` + `film-philosophy-bazin-tarkovsky.md` + `narrative-revolution-and-modernism.md` from `_shared/project-corpus/`

- **First-principles answer preview** (1 paragraph; the full derivation is in §3): the audience ultimately consumes a coherent emotional-cognitive experience that integrates narrative meaning + perceptual richness + emotional arc. This is NOT reducible to "video file" — a video file with no emotional arc is consumed as noise and forgotten.

- **Note on epistemic-status tags** that the §3 derivation will apply to claims about Q1's answer: mostly `psychological` (audience-reception is human-nature contingent but stable) with some `physical` (perceptual invariants like attention decay).

**§2.2 — Q2: What determines 短剧/微电影 quality?**

- Question framing: this question distinguishes good output from bad. Quality has multiple dimensions (Murch's Rule of Six: emotion, story, rhythm, eye-trace, planarity, spatial continuity) — which dimensions apply to 短剧/微电影, and how are they weighted?

- Sub-questions: "Is the 180° axis rule a `validated-invariant` (perceptual) or `contingent` (convention)?" "Does pacing matter more for 短剧 (where 完播率 gates distribution) than for 微电影 (where artistic merit gates festival selection)?" "Which of Murch's six dimensions are AIGC-weakest?"

- Corpus subset citation (per DERIV-07):
  - Primary: `04-后期/{editing-by-murch-rules, editing-rhythm-pacing, color-grading-strategy, final-mix, sound-layering-design}` + `03-拍摄/{cinematographer-masterclass, lighting-design, color-narrative-analysis}`
  - Secondary: `02-分镜/{cinematic-language-grammar, mise-en-scene-blocking}`
  - Hermes-integrated ready-to-cite: `cinematography-masterclass-and-grammar.md` + `editing-sound-post.md` + `lighting-equipment-and-design.md`
  - **GAP FLAG**: per STACK §1.4, the 102-book corpus is feature-film oriented; 短剧-specific quality drivers (前3秒 hook, 付费卡点 pacing, 竖屏 framing) are NOT in the corpus. Phase 7's Q2 answer must pair corpus with v1's `hook_retention/references/three-second-hooks.md` + external 短剧 sources. This gap surfaces for Phase 9 (corpus-traceability) to address.

- First-principles answer preview: quality is dominated by cross-node coherence (does the image match the story's tone? does the sound match the image's rhythm?) — this is PITFALLS 5.2's "coherence budget" insight. Per-node quality is necessary but not sufficient.

- Epistemic-status preview: mostly `psychological` (audience quality-perception is stable) with some `platform-algorithmic` (短剧 完播率 weighting is volatile).

**§2.3 — Q3: What can AI actually accelerate?**

- Question framing: this question identifies where AIGC marginal-value actually lies, NOT where we wish it lay. Per PITFALLS 1.3 + 2.7: avoid premature model-commitment; the answer must be at the user-value layer (composition lock, identity lock, pacing control) not the model layer (Sora 2, Kling).

- Sub-questions: "Which human craft operations are most procedural (low creative variance, high repetition)?" "Which operations are most expensive in human time (and therefore most cost-effective to AIGC-ize)?" "Which operations have the highest current-gen model capability ceiling?"

- Corpus subset citation (per DERIV-07):
  - Primary: NOT directly in 102-book corpus (corpus is pre-AIGC). INFER from `04-后期/` (which post tasks are most procedural? — color grading, foley layering, ADR) + `03-拍摄/animation-production.md` (animation is high-procedural + high-AIGC-friendly) + `05-制片/budget-allocation.md` (which human tasks are most expensive?).
  - Pair with kais-movie-agent V8 architecture (`/data/workspace/kais-movie-agent/docs/V8-ARCHITECTURE.md`) for actual AIGC integration points already tried
  - Hermes-integrated: `_shared/project-corpus/animation-disney-system.md` + `production-chinese-and-low-budget.md`
  - STACK §5 LLM-story-gen refs (8 papers) for the creative-story subset of Q3

- First-principles answer preview: AI accelerates (a) high-procedural post-production operations (color, foley, mix assist), (b) text-to-image/video generation where the spec is well-defined, (c) consistency verification (LLM-as-critic for script plot-holes, identity-check across shots). AI does NOT accelerate (d) creative intent origination (story kernel mining from lived experience), (e) final-cut judgment (the human "is this good?" call).

- Epistemic-status preview: `tool-capability` (current gen model limits — most volatile, monthly churn) + `psychological` (which operations humans find tedious — stable).

**§2.4 — Q4: What can AI never replace?**

- Question framing: this question sets the boundary that Phase 10 (LLM-Creative-Distillation) will work within. Per PITFALLS 1.2 + 5.3: do NOT discard validated invariants (Murch, 180° axis, three-act structure) as "bias" — they are compressed wisdom that any honest derivation re-discovers.

- Sub-questions: "Is creative intent reducible to a prompt?" "Can AI generate novel combinations outside training distribution?" "What does 'consistency' mean for fictional content (vs factual content)?" "Where does platform-vs-art tension live, and how does the design avoid dogma?"

- Corpus subset citation (per DERIV-07):
  - Primary: `06-理论批评/{film-philosophy-bazin, film-philosophy-tarkovsky, formalism-vs-realism}` (irreducible creative intent) + `01-剧本/{adaptation-writing, character-arc-design, dialogue-crafting}` (creative voice) + `03-拍摄/{acting-stanislavski-stella, actor-direction}` (performance truth)
  - Pair with book 麦基《故事》, 芦苇剧本笔记
  - Hermes-integrated ready-to-cite: `theory-formalism-vs-realism.md` + `film-philosophy-bazin-tarkovsky.md` + `narrative-revolution-and-modernism.md` + `screenwriting-chinese-and-supplementary.md`

- First-principles answer preview: AI cannot replace (a) the origination of creative intent from lived experience (this is what v1's `creative_source` expert mines), (b) the final artistic judgment call (theory_critic consultative per META-06), (c) the audience's emotional resonance with human-authored specificity (Bazin's "objectivity" argument). The boundary is not absolute — it shifts with model capability — but the design must mark which nodes are `AI-native` (no traditional precedent) vs `AI-augmented` (compresses traditional workflow) vs `AI-bounded` (AI cannot replace, only assist).

- Epistemic-status preview: `psychological` (creative intent is human-nature contingent) + `physical` (perceptual invariants like 180° axis).
